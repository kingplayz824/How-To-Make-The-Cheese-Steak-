The Story... The Cheese Steak Sandwich is a highly populzarized philadelphia made dish where you can find inside in door markets, actual restuarants or make it your self like I will be doing with you today! In the early 1930's a man created a glorious dish and went by the name of Patrick Oliveri. Patrick was or former owner to a small Hot Dog stand near the souther part of Philly. Pat wanted to try something diffrent from the same old Hot Dog so he invented something new using diffrent ingredients such as Onions, Melted Cheese, And sliced Beef. A taxi driver notced the sandwiches and asked to try some sparking the customer and his taste buds making huge popularity to this dish. Then the dishes popularity grew so much that created his owner restuarant called Pat's King of Steaks.

    RECIPE!

    1. Onions
    2. Provolone Cheese
    3. Defrosted Steak Ribeye
    4. Butter
    5. Hoagie Rolls
    6. Garlic & Jalapeno or Bell Peper (Optional)
    7. Season sliced ribeye with salt and pepper. Place a large skillet over medium heat and melt butter. Add sliced onion and cook until lightly caramelized, 6 to 8 minutes. Add sliced peppers and continue to cook until peppers soften, 3 to 4 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Transfer onion and peppers to a plate and return skillet to stove. Turn heat up to medium-high and add oil. Add beef to skillet and sear until just barely cooked through, 3 to 4 minutes. Add onions and peppers back to skillet and stir together until evenly distributed.
    8. Place 3 slices of provolone on top of the beef mixture, add 1-2 tablespoons water to the side of the skillet and cover for 1 minute, allowing the steam to melt the cheese. Stir mixture together, top with remaining slices of provolone, add 2 more tablespoons water to side of skillet and cover for another minute to melt cheese. Uncover and remove from heat.
    9. Spread mayonnaise onto each hoagie roll and top with 1/3 of the beef mixture. Serve immediately.
